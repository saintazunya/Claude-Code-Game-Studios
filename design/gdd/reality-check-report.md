# Reality Check: 一亩三分地 vs Game Actions

> **Method**: Ran 1000 games (10 agents × 100 games), logged 25,473 turn snapshots,
> sampled 100 diverse situations across 21 categories, then searched 一亩三分地 and
> Chinese immigrant forums for what real people do in each situation.

## Executive Summary

The game covers ~60% of real-world actions well. Three systemic gaps appear across
nearly every crisis scenario:

1. **Day-1 CPT / back to school** — the #1 emergency escape valve, missing entirely
2. **Interview during PIP** — game blocked this, real world it's the first thing people do (FIXED)
3. **FMLA / medical leave** — the key burnout survival tool for H1B holders, missing

## Category-by-Category Findings

### Crisis Situations

| Category | Real #1 Action | In Game? | Key Gap |
|----------|---------------|----------|---------|
| H1B denied | Day-1 CPT (back to school) | **ADDED** | O-1 visa path, Canada transfer |
| Laid off on H1B | Urgent job search + H1B transfer | ✅ | B-2 conversion, severance negotiation |
| PIP | Start interviewing immediately | **FIXED** | Was blocked by precondition |
| Visa expiring | Pressure employer + NIW backup | Partial | No "push employer" action |
| OPT unemployed | Desperate search + self-employment | ✅ | No freelance/self-employ |
| Burnout | FMLA (12-week leave) | ❌ | Major gap — no medical leave |
| Recession employed | Work super hard + emergency fund | ✅ | No "survival job" (ICC) |

### Career Situations
