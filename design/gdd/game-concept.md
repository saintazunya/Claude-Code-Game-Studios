# Game Concept: Green Card Odyssey (绿卡之路)

*Created: 2026-03-28*
*Status: Draft*

---

## Elevator Pitch

> A turn-based life simulator where you play as a first-generation Chinese
> immigrant navigating the American dream — from college graduation to
> retirement. Balance career advancement, visa timelines, financial strategy,
> and mental health across 148 quarterly turns, with the goal of securing a
> green card before age 59 while maximizing your net worth. One wrong move —
> or one unlucky H1B lottery — and you're on a plane home.

## Core Fantasy

You are a first-generation Chinese immigrant standing at the starting line of
the American dream with nothing but a degree and ambition. Every month is a
chess move — allocate your limited time and energy across career, immigration
status, finances, and sanity. The system is stacked against you: visa lotteries
are random, layoffs come without warning, green card queues stretch for years,
and the clock never stops ticking.

The fantasy is **mastering an unfair system through smart strategy** — finding
the optimal path through a maze that millions of real people navigate every day.
When you finally see that green card approval, you earned it.

---

## Unique Hook

Like "Chinese Parents," AND ALSO the stakes are your literal right to stay in
the country. Every career decision, every financial choice, every month you
spend (or don't spend) on immigration paperwork cascades through a web of
real visa rules, employer dependencies, and bureaucratic timelines. The tension
isn't abstract — it maps to a journey millions of people actually live.

## Player Experience Analysis (MDA Framework)

### Target Aesthetics (What the player FEELS)

| Aesthetic | Priority | How We Deliver It |
| ---- | ---- | ---- |
| **Sensation** | 6 | Clean, satisfying UI feedback on decisions; monthly results feel impactful |
| **Fantasy** | 3 | Live a compressed version of the immigrant journey with all its highs and lows |
| **Narrative** | 4 | Emergent stories from random events + player decisions; no scripted plot |
| **Challenge** | 1 | Tight resource constraints, random disruptions, multiple fail states |
| **Fellowship** | N/A | Single-player experience |
| **Discovery** | 2 | Multiple career paths, visa strategies, hidden optimal routes to explore across runs |
| **Expression** | 5 | Different viable strategies (academic vs industry, risk-averse vs aggressive) |
| **Submission** | N/A | This game is intentionally stressful, not relaxing |

### Key Dynamics (Emergent player behaviors)

- Players will theorycraft optimal career-visa-finance paths across multiple runs
- Players will weigh "safe" H1B→EB2 path against riskier but faster O1/EB1 routes
- Players will develop timing intuitions: when to jump jobs, when to hold for green card
- Players will share their unique "immigration stories" generated by each run
- Players will feel genuine relief when the green card is approved — or genuine frustration at a bad lottery

### Core Mechanics (Systems we build)

1. **Quarterly action point allocation** — 10 base AP per quarter; choose work mode (躺平2/正常4/卷王6) then allocate remaining AP to other actions. 卷王 grants +3 bonus AP (total 13) at health/mental cost.
2. **Attribute-driven probability system** — 7 core attributes (绩效/技能/学术影响力/人脉/健康/精神/财务) that accumulate over time and feed into probability rolls for all key events (promotion, job hop, visa approval, sickness, PIP).
3. **Career progression system** — job roles, levels (L3→L7), promotions, PIP risk, job hopping. All outcomes probability-based: higher 绩效 → higher promotion chance, but never guaranteed.
4. **Immigration status state machine** — two major tracks: (a) company-sponsored PERM→I-140→I-485 (passive, no AP cost, but employer-bound) vs (b) self-directed NIW/EB1A/O1 (active, costs AP, requires 学术影响力 accumulation, but employer-independent). PERM has critical I-140 checkpoint: pre-I-140 job change = total reset; post-I-140 = priority date preserved.
5. **Economic engine** — salary, expenses, S&P 500 auto-invest (no AP cost), real estate. Passive income systems run automatically; AP spent on active decisions only.
6. **Health + 卷王 system** — health decays with overwork, recovers with rest/travel. Sickness probability = (100 - health) × 0.6%. Sickness events cost AP, money, and force 卷王 cooldown. Creates debt cycle: borrowed AP now → pay back with interest later.
7. **Economic cycle system** — boom/recession/recovery phases affect layoff rates, PERM processing, job market, and investment returns simultaneously. Players must adapt strategy to macro conditions.

## Player Motivation Profile

### Primary Psychological Needs Served

| Need | How This Game Satisfies It | Strength |
| ---- | ---- | ---- |
| **Autonomy** | Every month presents meaningful choices with real tradeoffs; multiple viable strategies exist | Core |
| **Competence** | System mastery across runs; first run is survival, fifth run is optimization | Core |
| **Relatedness** | Emotional resonance with real immigrant experience; "this is my life" moments | Supporting |

### Player Type Appeal (Bartle Taxonomy)

- [x] **Achievers** — Optimize net worth score, find fastest green card path, unlock all career routes
- [x] **Explorers** — Discover different visa strategies, career paths, and system interactions across runs
- [ ] **Socializers** — Not primary (single-player), but sharing stories adds social layer
- [ ] **Killers/Competitors** — Leaderboard score comparison (secondary)

### Flow State Design

- **Onboarding curve**: First playthrough starts with guided first year (tutorial), introduces systems one at a time (career first, then visa, then finance, then mental health)
- **Difficulty scaling**: Early years have fewer decision branches; complexity compounds as career advances and visa timelines converge
- **Feedback clarity**: Monthly summary shows all stat changes; annual review shows year-over-year progress; clear indicators for visa deadlines
- **Recovery from failure**: Deportation = game over with score, but quick restart encourages "one more run". Mid-game setbacks (H1B denial) are recoverable if you have backup plans

## Core Loop

### Moment-to-Moment (30 seconds)
Each quarter: review your status dashboard (visa countdown, finances, career
level, health, mental state) → optionally toggle 卷王 mode → allocate 8-10
action points across categories → confirm → see results + any random events
→ react to events with a choice → next quarter.

The satisfaction comes from the *decision itself* — weighing "do I spend this
quarter pushing for promotion, or preparing immigration paperwork?" when both
feel urgent. Quarterly cadence means each turn is heavier — one bad quarter
can cascade.

### Short-Term (5-15 minutes)
Annual milestones create natural mini-goals (every 4 turns):
- Q2 each year: H1B lottery season — did you prepare enough?
- Q4 each year: Annual performance review — promotion or PIP?
- Visa expiration deadlines — do you have a backup plan?
- Investment milestones — market boom or crash?

"One more year" psychology: "If I can just get through this lottery cycle..."

### Session-Level (40-70 minutes)
A full playthrough covers age 22-59 (148 quarterly turns). The player watches
their entire immigrant life unfold — career arc, financial growth, visa drama.
Session ends with a score screen showing:
- Final net worth
- Green card status (and when obtained, if ever)
- Career peak (highest level reached)
- Life timeline visualization with key moments

### Long-Term Progression
- Unlock new career paths after completing runs
- Discover hidden strategies (O1 visa route, EB1 shortcut, startup path)
- Chase higher scores on leaderboard
- Meta-knowledge: understanding the system better each run

### Retention Hooks
- **Curiosity**: "What if I went the PhD route instead?" / "What if I tried O1?"
- **Investment**: Score history, best runs, career paths discovered
- **Social**: Shareable end-game summary cards ("My immigrant story in one image")
- **Mastery**: Optimizing for highest net worth, fastest green card, completing all paths

## Game Pillars

### Pillar 1: Authenticity First (真实优先)
Every mechanic must map to a real immigrant experience. If it doesn't happen in
real life, it doesn't happen in the game. Visa rules, salary ranges, market
returns, green card timelines — all grounded in reality.

*Design test*: "Should we add a mechanic where you can bribe USCIS?" No —
it doesn't reflect the real experience. Rejected by Pillar 1.

### Pillar 2: Every Choice Costs (每个选择都有代价)
No free lunches. Job hopping raises salary but may reset green card priority
date. Buying a house builds equity but locks your cash. Spending time on mental
health costs action points you could use for career. Ignoring mental health
costs you even more later.

*Design test*: "Should getting a promotion be purely positive?" No — it must
come with higher expectations, PIP risk, and stress. Every gain has a cost.

### Pillar 3: Time Is the Ultimate Currency (时间是终极货币)
Visa deadlines, age limits, green card queue years, career windows — the player
must always feel that time is running out. There is never enough time to do
everything. Prioritization IS the game.

*Design test*: "Can the player just grind indefinitely?" No — every system has
a ticking clock. The 59-year-old deadline is absolute.

### Pillar 4: Emergent Stories (涌现叙事)
No scripted storyline. The story emerges from the collision of random events and
player decisions. Two playthroughs should produce two completely different
"immigration stories." The game is a story generator, not a story teller.

*Design test*: "Should we write a fixed narrative arc?" No — invest in the
event pool and systemic interactions instead.

### Pillar 5: Learnable Depth (可学习的深度)
First run: survival mode, learning the systems. Fifth run: optimizing strategy,
exploiting system interactions. The game rewards players who study and
experiment, just like the real immigration system rewards those who research
their options.

*Design test*: "Should we simplify the visa system so casual players aren't
overwhelmed?" No — but we should teach it incrementally. Complexity is the
feature, not a bug.

### Anti-Pillars (What This Game Is NOT)

- **NOT an action game**: No reflexes, no timing challenges, no twitch gameplay. Pure strategy. This protects mobile-friendliness and accessibility.
- **NOT a visual novel**: No branching dialogue trees, no romance routes, no walls of text. Events are short, impactful, and choice-driven.
- **NOT multiplayer**: Single-player focus. No social features that gate progression. Leaderboards are the only social element.
- **NOT morally prescriptive**: The game does not judge player choices. Staying on expired visa, taking a lower job, prioritizing money over wellness — the game shows consequences, not moral verdicts.

## Risks and Open Questions

### Design Risks
- **Monthly turns may feel repetitive** — mitigation: strong event variety, phase-specific mechanics, meaningful annual milestones
- **Pure number management could feel like a spreadsheet** — mitigation: strong UI/UX design, event narratives inject emotion, visual progression feedback
- **Balancing multiple viable strategies is hard** — mitigation: start with one path (SDE + H1B), validate, then expand

### Technical Risks
- **Web + mobile performance with Godot 4** — Godot's web export has improved but needs testing on mobile browsers
- **Economic simulation balance** — salary curves, market returns, and housing prices need careful calibration to real-world data
- **State machine complexity** — visa transitions have many edge cases; needs thorough modeling

### Market Risks
- **Niche audience** — Chinese immigrant experience is specific; broader appeal depends on universal themes of struggle and strategy
- **Sensitive topic** — immigration politics could attract unwanted attention; neutral, factual tone is essential
- **Competition from Chinese Parents-style games** — differentiation through the immigration hook is key

### Scope Risks
- **Event content volume** — 50-80 events is a lot of writing; prioritize quality over quantity for MVP
- **Career path variety** — each path needs its own balance tuning; start with 1-2, not all 7
- **Mobile UI complexity** — monthly turns with multiple systems need clean, intuitive mobile interface

### Open Questions
- **Exact action point economy**: How many points per month? What's the cost of each action? — Needs prototyping
- **Green card timeline modeling**: How closely should we mirror real EB2/EB3 wait times? Compress for gameplay or keep realistic? — Needs playtesting
- **Single run length**: 148 quarterly turns (37 years) — is this the right pacing? Should we allow starting at different ages? — Needs prototyping
- **Monetization model**: Premium ($5-10) vs free-to-play? — Decide after MVP validation
- **Localization**: Chinese + English from day one, or English first? — Decide based on primary audience

## MVP Definition

**Core hypothesis**: Players find the quarterly decision loop engaging for a full
40-70 minute session, AND the attribute-probability system creates meaningful
strategy depth where effort improves odds but never guarantees outcomes.

**Required for MVP**:
1. Quarterly turn loop with 10 base AP + work mode selection (躺平/正常/卷王)
2. 7 core attributes (绩效/技能/学术影响力/人脉/健康/精神/财务) with accumulation and decay
3. Probability-driven outcomes for all key events (promotion, job hop, visa, sickness, PIP)
4. One complete career path: SDE (L3 → L7), with attribute-based promotion odds
5. Core visa pipeline: F1 → OPT → H1B (lottery) → PERM → I-140 → I-485, with I-140 checkpoint logic
6. Basic economic system: salary, auto-invest S&P 500, house buying (passive, no AP cost)
7. Health/卷王 system: sickness probability scaling, forced recovery, 卷王 cooldown
8. 20-30 random events + economic cycle (boom/recession)
9. Game over conditions: deportation (visa expiry with no backup) or reaching age 59
10. Score screen: net worth + green card status + career peak + timeline

**Explicitly NOT in MVP** (defer to later):
- Additional career paths (MLE, DS, RS, academic, startup)
- Self-directed immigration routes (NIW, EB1A, O1)
- Social/networking attribute actions
- Narrative life events (family, cultural moments)
- Leaderboard / sharing features
- Sound and music
- Polished art (use placeholder/minimal UI)

### Scope Tiers

| Tier | Content | Features | Timeline |
| ---- | ---- | ---- | ---- |

| **Vertical Slice** | 3 career paths, 3 visa routes, 50 events | Core + investment + mental health polish | 10-12 weeks |
